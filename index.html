Welcome to HOUDINI-BD

HOUDINI-BD (High-resolution Ongoing Unsupervised Data-efficient Identification of Novelties within Individuals – Bipolar Disorder) is a research study sponsored by Imperial College London and funded by NIHR Imperial Biomedical Research Centre and Imperial College London Faculty of Medicine. The study protocol was approved by the relevant ethical committee.

The Study

Bipolar disorder is a common mental health condition characterized by significant shifts in mood, energy, and sleep patterns during recurring episodes, interspersed with periods of relative stability. One of the main challenges in care is that changes in mood, energy, and sleep often begin between clinic visits, making new episodes difficult to detect and address early.

The "Blood Sugar" Analogy
At present, there is no simple, objective test for monitoring bipolar disorder—nothing comparable to the finger-prick blood glucose test used in diabetes care. As a result, early warning signs can be missed, thus limiting opportunities for timely support or intervention.

A Modern Solution
Smart wristbands (such as smartwatches) offer a potential way to bridge this gap. By tracking sleep and activity levels continuously and with minimal effort, they provide an ongoing view of everyday patterns. Subtle changes in these rhythms may signal that a person’s mental state is beginning to shift.

The HOUDINI-BD Mission
HOUDINI-BD will be exploring whether this type of technology can be a practical and reliable way to support the monitoring of bipolar disorder in daily life. The study is observational and does not provide diagnosis or treatment. We focus on two main areas:

  • People: Working closely with “experts by experience” (people with bipolar disorder, family members, and healthcare staff) to understand whether the technology is comfortable, acceptable, and useful in real-world settings.
  • Technology: Developing and testing analytical methods that aim to identify meaningful patterns in activity and sleep data.

Take Part

Have you been diagnosed with bipolar disorder and experienced any bipolar episodes in the past two years?

If so, you may be eligible to join our study and help us develop a system for the early detection of new bipolar episodes from physiological/behavioural data, collected with a wristband. Your participation could provide key insights that help improve future care and support. As a token of appreciation for your time and contribution, we will provide £30.00 after each study appointment.

If you are interested in joining, the first step is to complete our brief screening questionnaire at this link.

You can find detailed information about what is involved in our Participant Information Sheet A shorter version is provided here . We encourage you to read carefully before deciding whether to take part. If you have any questions at all, please feel free to reach out to the study team using the details in the Contact section below.


What happens next?

If you are eligible after screening and choose to participate, we will arrange a baseline appointment (60–70 minutes) with you at West London NHS Trust, 1 Armstrong Way, Southall UB2 4SD .

During this visit, you will have a chance to ask any questions you may have. If you agree to take part, we will ask you to sign a consent form and we will collect some information about your mental health history and demographics (e.g., age, sex, occupation). We will then provide you with a smart wristband to wear during your daily life over the next 12 months.

To see how you are getting on, we will arrange four quarterly follow-up appointments (30–40 minutes each) at the same location in Southall.


Cohort & Progress

As the HOUDINI-BD study progresses, we will herewith share updates on the study cohort (e.g. total number of participants joined, hours of wristband datarecorded, cohort age and sex distribution, etc...) and research findings.

Participants' Personalized Summary
To keep participants informed about their contribution, we will periodically email them with a personalized summary of the sleep-wake patterns and energy levels recorded by the wristband. This summary is intended to help particiapants better understand their sleep-wake and energy levels patterns; it is provided for information only and does not constitute medical advice.
